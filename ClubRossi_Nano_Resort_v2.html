The Vision

Club Rossi

A Nano Resort in Lake Tahoe.

What you have at 3010 Electric is not a cabin. It's a destination — a main house, a clubhouse, a studio, grounds with real potential, and a setting that very few properties in Tahoe can match. The Property · Three Distinct Zones

Main House. Clubhouse. Grounds.

Most properties compete on bedrooms and bathrooms. Club Rossi competes on something harder to replicate — a full destination experience across three distinct zones. Guests don't just stay here. They inhabit it.

The Anchor
Main House
Primary guest experience. Four beds, living, kitchen, deck. The spaces that every booking turns on — where the property earns its first impression and its last one.
The Differentiator
Clubhouse
Entertainment suite, sleeping accommodation, and flex space above the garage. The amenity that separates a destination from a rental — and the reason guests bring friends on the second visit.
The Arrival
Grounds
The arrival experience — the first and last thing guests see and feel. Pool, sports court, alpine plantings, fire pit, sauna. The grounds are where the resort signal lives.

The Full Vision · Zone-by-Zone

Where the experience begins.

Walked zone by zone, from the front elevation to the clubhouse above the garage. Ten pieces that together become a single destination — the grounds set the stage, the interior carries the story, and the clubhouse is the amenity no other property on Electric Avenue has.

Zone A · Landscaping
Sierra / Alpine Plantings
Granite boulder anchors, alpine plant palette — sedum, thyme, penstemon, kinnikinnick — pea gravel and decomposed granite paths. Materials that read as if they came from the ground beneath the house.
Zone A · Materials
Three Sierra Materials
Granite, pea gravel, decomposed granite. All locally sourced. No concrete pavers, no imported bluestone, nothing that fights the setting. The ground reads as if it was always this way.
Zone B · Sports Court
Modular Tile Court
Modular tile surface, seasonal, removable fencing. Optional basketball hoop for half-court play. Comes up before the snow — no plow conflict, no freeze-thaw cost cycle.
Zone C · Outdoor Dining
Upper Deck · Intentional Furniture
Upper deck reset with intentional outdoor dining furniture, a shade structure, and sightlines held toward the pines. A meal happens here every night of a booking — the space should earn that.
Zone D · Wellness
Sauna + Hot Tub
Both are already on the property. Restored to full function and dialed in as a wellness pairing — a core Tahoe amenity that guests now expect and that meaningfully lifts shoulder-season bookings.
Zone E · Gathering
Fire Pit Terrace
Steel ring, pea gravel circle, propane feed from the house. The evening gathering anchor — the photograph every guest takes on their first night, and the reason they linger outside.
Zone F · Pool
Mod Pools · 8′ × 20′ Container
Above-grade container pool with ipe lounge deck, visible from the front drive. Instantly signals resort. Uses winter covers and a hot spot heater — functional three seasons, dramatic year-round.
Zone G · Lawn Play
Chipping & Putting Green
Two-tier installation on the east lawn. Low-maintenance synthetic turf, real cup, pitch from anywhere on the property. A guest differentiator that reads luxury without reading country club.
Zone H · Interior
Full Interior Design
Right now, the main house has strong bones and an inconsistent story. The move: a single narrative rather than room-by-room decisions — palette, lighting, art, and rugs working together as one decision rather than four. Architecture, window line, and hearth all stay. The goal is narrative cohesion, not reinvention.
Zone I · Clubhouse
Upper Suite · Entertainment & Guest
The upstairs unit above the garage, reimagined: a proper en suite bedroom, a lounge that reads as a gathering room, and a bar-height entertaining bench. The amenity that turns a rental into a destination — and the reason guests bring friends on their second visit.
Zone J · Game Room
A Room Planned Around You
Two directions, one idea: a movie room with tiered seating and blackout treatment — or an arcade, pinball, and simulator suite. Either way, it's the room guests talk about when they get home. The second-booking amenity.
The Bridge · From Vision to Phase One

Phase One · What we recommend now.

That's the destination. We're not asking you to build all of that right now — and we're not suggesting you need to. What we roughed this out for is simple: before recommending anything for the next ninety days, we needed to know that nothing we touch now creates work you'd have to undo later. Everything in Phase One that follows is deliberately chosen because it advances this vision, whether or not you ever go further. If this is all you ever did, the property would still be a meaningful step forward. But it also sets the table cleanly for what comes next.

Slide 2.3 · Item 03
Item 03 · Owner Priority

A sports court that survives Tahoe winters.

$15,000 – $25,000 dependent on leveling & optional hoop
  • Modular tile sports courtRemovable, seasonal, designed for alpine climate. Comes up before the snow — no plow conflict, no freeze-thaw damage cycle.
  • Removable perimeter fencingIncluded in estimate. Goes up and down with the court every spring and fall.
  • Optional · Basketball hoopPermanent or removable, for half-court configuration. Negligible added cost.
  • Leveling assessmentRange reflects existing grade. Minor pad work is within the stated number; major re-grading would require a separate conversation.
Mark, you asked us to look at the pickleball court. Here's our honest recommendation: resurfacing the existing surface would look great for one season and need redoing after the first plow. The snow, the plow, the freeze-thaw cycle — it would be a recurring cost. A modular tile system goes in at the start of the season, comes up before the snow, and lasts. It's the right call for this climate, and the fencing bundles into the same estimate.
Slide 2.4 · Item 04
Item 04 · Quick Win

A $3–5K fix for an amenity guests already expect.

$3,000 – $5,000
  • Insulation upgrade beneath the saunaThe floor is losing heat, which means the existing unit is overworking. Targeted insulation brings it back to spec.
  • Specialist assessmentConfirm whether the floor repair alone is sufficient, or whether the heating unit itself needs replacement. Range spans both outcomes.
  • Hot tub · No action neededFunctioning as-is. Paired with the sauna, this becomes a full wellness pairing at zero additional cost.
The sauna is already there and guests will absolutely use it. This is a targeted fix that restores it to full function — and at this price point, it's one of the highest-return items on the list.
